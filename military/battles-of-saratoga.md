# Battles of Saratoga

**Category**: Military Events / **Key figures**: General John Burgoyne (British), Major General Horatio Gates (American), Major General Benedict Arnold (American), Colonel Daniel Morgan (American)

## Summary

The Battles of Saratoga comprised two engagements fought near Saratoga, New York, in the autumn of 1777. The first battle, at Freeman's Farm on September 19, 1777, saw British General John Burgoyne's army of approximately 6,000 troops clash with American forces under Major General Horatio Gates. Benedict Arnold commanded the American left flank, and Colonel Daniel Morgan's riflemen inflicted heavy casualties on the British before Burgoyne held the field at significant cost. On October 7, 1777, Burgoyne launched a second attack at Bemis Heights; Arnold led an aggressive American counterattack while Morgan's riflemen enveloped the British rear, collapsing the British line. Burgoyne opened surrender negotiations on October 13 and formally surrendered his army of approximately 5,900 men to Gates on October 17, 1777 — the first capitulation of a British field army in the war.

American forces suffered roughly 330 casualties across both engagements; British casualties totaled approximately 1,135 killed and wounded, with more than 6,200 taken prisoner upon the surrender.

## Significance

The American victory at Saratoga is widely regarded as the turning point of the Revolutionary War. The surrender of an entire British army convinced France — which had been providing covert aid — to enter into a formal military alliance with the United States, signed in February 1778, fundamentally altering the strategic balance of the war. The victory also boosted American morale, demonstrated the viability of the Continental Army against British regulars, and helped secure the international support that proved essential to ultimate American independence.
